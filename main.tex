\begin{frame}[plain]
\contentheader{What the Book Adds Beyond Price}
\begin{columns}[T,totalwidth=\textwidth]
\begin{column}{0.52\textwidth}
\begin{itemize}\tightitems
  \item A LOB records resting buy and sell queues across price levels.
  \item A typical model input is a recent history of the first \(L\) levels:
  \[
  X_t\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times4L}.
  \]
  \item Queue depletion explains why the next price change happens.
  \item Queue imbalance gives a first-order pressure measure:
  \[
  I_t^{(k)}=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^k q^b_i-\sum_{i=1}^k q^a_i}{\sum_{i=1}^k q^b_i+\sum_{i=1}^k q^a_i}.
  \]
\end{itemize}
\end{column}

\begin{frame}[plain]
\contentheader{Liquidity Is the Tradable State}
\begin{columns}[T,totalwidth=\textwidth]
\begin{column}{0.50\textwidth}
\begin{block}{A trade is viable only when}
\[
E[\Delta p] > spread + fee + impact + delay\ risk.
\]
\end{block}
\begin{itemize}\tightitems
  \item Spread measures immediate trading friction.
  \item Depth and impact determine capacity.
  \item Resiliency determines how quickly the book recovers.
  \item Slippage is the operational truth of the signal.
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
